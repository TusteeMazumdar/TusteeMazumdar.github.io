### ClimateNet: A Multi-Faceted Classifier for Analyzing Climate Change Discourse  

**Authors:** **Tustee Mazumdar**, Adrika Chowdhury, Abu Nowshed Chy, Tarem Ahmed  

**Conference:** *IEEE R10 Humanitarian Technology Conference (HTC), 2025*  

---

**Abstract:**  
Climate change discourse on social media contains diverse signals such as stance, relevance, humor, and hate speech, which are crucial for understanding public opinion and guiding policy decisions. This paper introduces **ClimateNet**, a hybrid neural architecture that combines transformer-based models (BERT, RoBERTa, DistilBERT, ClimateBERT) with BiLSTM and an MLP classifier to perform multi-dimensional classification of climate-related text. Experimental results demonstrate that ClimateNet significantly outperforms existing state-of-the-art models across multiple tasks, providing deeper insights into online climate discussions and supporting sustainable development goals.
